Custodian Jacket

A practical outerwear garment designed to carry story, respect, movement and everyday function.

Developed to explore how clothing can support cultural expression while remaining wearable, useful and accountable.

Designed with restraint. Earthy colour, controlled artwork placement and QR-linked attribution keep the garment practical, respectful and connected to its story.

Acknowledgement

Acknowledgement of Country

I acknowledge the Darug people as the Traditional Custodians of the land on which the Custodian Jacket was developed, and I pay my respects to Elders past and present.

I also acknowledge the guidance shared through the Darug Custodian Aboriginal Corporation and Muru Mittigar. Their cultural guidance helped shape the way this project approached consultation, attribution, visual restraint and respect.

The Custodian Jacket has been developed with the understanding that cultural representation must be guided, acknowledged and treated with care.

Origin

The Story Behind the Jacket

The Custodian Jacket began with a question:

How can clothing express identity respectfully, while still being practical enough to wear every day?

The design was shaped by research into First Nations fashion, everyday outerwear, cultural representation, storage, comfort, movement and sustainability. Feedback showed that people wanted more clothing that could support Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander identity expression, but not in a way that felt tokenistic, uncomfortable or impractical.

The jacket responds to this by combining cultural communication with practical garment features. It includes hidden side-seam zip pockets, an internal mesh pocket, a relaxed unisex fit, side adjustment, movement-friendly construction, reinforced seams, a hood-to-bag feature and a QR attribution label.

The final design uses earthy colours, simple branding and controlled artwork placement so the garment remains wearable and respectful rather than visually crowded.

Testing

End-User Feedback

The final jacket was tested by an anonymous Aboriginal end-user in an outdoor Sydney CBD setting.

He wore the jacket and tested front fit, arm movement, reaching, pocket access, comfort, visual impression and QR scan access.

This feedback supported the decision to keep the jacket restrained, practical and low-bulk rather than adding heavy decoration or oversized external pockets.

The pockets are easy to get to and do not stick out.
It looks practical, but it still has something different about it.

Attribution

QR Attribution Label

The QR code label is part of the jacket's communication system.

It is placed inside the garment beside the care label, so it is protected but still easy to find. When scanned, the QR code opens this page and connects the wearer or viewer to the garment story, acknowledgement, care instructions, material information and testing background.

The QR label means the jacket does not rely only on what is visible on the outside. It allows the story, care and attribution to stay attached to the garment in a practical way.

Materials

Materials and Sustainability

The Custodian Jacket uses a ripstop/DWR outer shell for durability and light weather resistance.

The final garment also includes reinforced seams, hidden zip pockets, an internal mesh pocket, webbing, zips, hardware, fabric paint and a QR label.

The jacket is lifecycle-aware rather than fully sustainable. Some performance materials, such as coated or synthetic outerwear fabrics, create environmental trade-offs. To reduce short-life fashion risk, the design prioritises durability, repair-conscious construction, careful cutting, offcut awareness and clear care instructions.

The aim is to create a garment that is more likely to be kept, cared for and used over time.

Development

Development Note

The Custodian Jacket is a tested prototype and design investigation, not a commercial release or mass-produced garment.

Further development would require broader fit testing, repeated wear trials, long-term wash testing, continued cultural consultation, professional waterproof testing and further investigation into lower-impact outerwear materials.
